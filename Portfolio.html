Shortly about SayarGyi U Pe Maung Tin


             SayarGyi U Pe Maung Tin was born in Insein, in 1888.He was a scholar of Pali and Buddhism and educator in Myanmar, formerly Burma. He was from an Anglican family . He was the fifth child of U Pe and Daw Myaing. His grandfather was the first Burmese pastor of Henzada. He learnt the basic Buddhist texts at a local private school before he went to Rangoon Government High School where he won a scholarship at age 14.He won lots of Scholarships in his student life.He graduated with a B. A. degree from University College, Rangoon in 1909 and an M. A. degree from the University of Calcutta in 1911. Pe Maung Tin became the first national professor of Pali language at University College, Rangoon, and also, at the age of 24, the youngest professor in Burma in 1912.

      He was the only Burmese to be appointed to the commission set up in 1918 to establish Rangoon University independent of Calcutta. He then worked hard to raise the status of Burmese literature as an Honours degree subject. He himself brought out texts to teach. The first course was taught in 1924, and the first student was Sein Tin who graduated in 1927 and later became famous under the pen name Theippan Maung Wa.

            He contributed to various language debates held by the Society (1922–1925). The year 1934 saw the publication of Hkit san pon byin ( Experimental Tales), a collection of short stories in a new style of writing, by his students Sayar Theippan Maung Wa,Sayar Zawgyi and Sayar Min Thu Wun, followed by an anthology called Hkit san kabya ( Experimental Poems). Professor Pe Maung Tin explained in his foreword that he coined the word hkit san ( testing the times) as he wanted his students to experiment and test the reaction of the readers. He was also an accomplished musician and had helped sponsor the Rangoon College Philharmonic Society.

            He became principal of University College, Rangoon University, in 1937. In 1939 he was elected president of the Burma Research Society. After the Second World War in 1946, he retired at age 58. He was however asked to serve as principal of the University of Adult Education in 1947. He then became chairman of the University Translation and Publication Advisory Board and professor emeritus of Pali the following year. He was re-elected president of the BRS in 1950, and awarded an honorary doctorate LL. D. by Rangoon University in 1952.

            From 1960 to 1964, He served as chairman of the Burma Historical Commission. He led the Burma Translation Society in compiling the Burmese Encyclopedia. In 1968 the BRS marked his 80th birthday with a special celebration. The Pali Text Society in 1972 requested him to become their editor for the 50 volume publication of the Atthakatha, the year before he died in 1973 at age 85.

Shortly about U Chit Maung


      U Chit Maung was born in Okpho, Thayarwady District,in 1913. He studied at Latpatan High School. After he finished high school, he started work for Rangoon newspapers.He didn't join the College.

      He was a journalist.He was Chief Editor of myanmar Alin newspaper. Later his own Journal Kyaw Newspaper became well known in Burma. He actively participated in General Aung San's Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League.

      He wife, Sayarma Journal Kyaw Mama Lay was a one of the well-known female authors in our country.She is also one of my favorite authors. U Chit Maung's eldest daughter,Dr. Daw Khin Lay Myint was a noted French scholar who died in 2007. She translated her mother's works into French and some French classics into Burmese.His son U Maung Thein Tan became an actor. His youngest son was the poet U Moe Hein. Due to poor health, he died at the age of 33. His wife Sayarma Journal Kyaw Ma Ma Lay wrote her husband's biography that is called "Thu LoLu".
